## Hansun at the Crater / Aumonier Dialogue (lines 349-500ish)

**Verdict: TIGHTEN SIGNIFICANTLY. Cut 30%.**

### What works:
- The visual of the crater — "a thin slurry at the base of the crater, membrane-thick and almost perfectly lenticular, as if the entire complex had turned to liquid." This is brilliantly horrible.
- "I'm not known for my sense of humour."
- The political subtext about Arco.
- "The star's trying to kill us" — Hansun's best interior line.
- The logic chain: gravity weapon → siphoned stellar plasma → wormholes → alien technology → we have access.

### What doesn't work:
- **Too much back-and-forth on technical details.** The physics of the gravity weapon is explained twice — once by Aumonier, once by Hansun restating it. Cut the restatement.
- **Hansun defers too much.** Four "Yes, sir" moments. Cut to two.
- **The Mimics reference is throwaway.** "The Mimics can, perhaps, achieve the energy densities..." This is the only mention of the aliens and it's dismissive. Fine for here — the Mimics matter later — but note: when we reach the base life scenes, the reader should remember this dismissal and realize how wrong they were.
- **The scene takes too long to reach the survivor discovery.** The survivor reveal is the hook that pulls us into the main story. Currently buried under technical briefing. Restructure: crater description → Aumonier's one key political point → the alert → the survivor image. Move the extended physics discussion to a later scene or cut it.

### Rewrite approach:
1. Open with Hansun at the crater. 3-4 paragraphs of visual horror. His tremor.
2. Aumonier (holographic) — brief exchange. Key beats: gravity weapon confirmed, unknown agency, "imagine if we'd had this at Threshold."
3. The alert. The survivor image. Hansun's shock. "They're fifteen hundred kilometres from—" "Get over there."
4. Cut: extended physics of the weapon, repeated deferrals, the drone/legionnaire scene-setting.

## Hansun Retrieves Eszel (lines 500-593)

**Verdict: GOOD. Minor tightening.**

### What works:
- The flight across Kailash on the flyer. The landscape. Hansun's interiority: "Perhaps it's my fate to be brought in to salvage fuckups."
- "It's small. A child?" — The moment of realization.
- "I thought I was dead" — Eszel's first words. Perfect.

### What doesn't work:
- Hansun's worry on the flight repeats the crater scene's worry. He's already established as anxious. On the flight, shift his emotional register — now it's curiosity and dread, not just fear.
- The legionnaires are described twice (once at the crater, once here). The second description should be minimal.

### Specific fixes:
- Cut "Would there be a sign? Would I feel my body being torn apart before the end?" — We've already had this beat. He's afraid of the weapon. Got it.
- Expand the moment Eszel meets his eyes. Currently: "The girl met his eyes, face blank. The moment seemed to stretch." This is the first meeting between our two POV characters. Slow down. What does Hansun see? Not just blankness — something specific. Eszel looking AT him rather than TOWARD him. An evaluating gaze. Something that makes him uncomfortable before he can articulate why.
- The crying: "The girl burst into tears." Good — it's the performance. But let it be slightly wrong somehow. She bursts into tears a beat too late, or her body language doesn't match. Hansun shouldn't notice consciously, but the reader should.

## Scenes C1/C2: First Contact / The Choice (NEW)

**Verdict: EXTENSIVE REWRITE NEEDED. This is the weakest section.**

Per the judge report (scored 6/10 on style):
1. Eszel is too emotionally available, too moved, too receptive
2. AI prose tells: italicized emphasis, triplet structures, rhythmic regularity
3. "Walking deeper into the body of a sleeping god" — purple prose
4. The choice is over-explained. "I did not want to go back" — Eszel wouldn't state this directly
5. The Mimics are too immediately communicative; the original suggests a more gradual, stranger contact

### Rewrite principles for C1:
- Eszel goes out for the WRONG reasons (curiosity, the pull) not the RIGHT ones
- The Mimic contact should be perceptual, not verbal — geometries, patterns, not propositions
- Her reaction should be intellectual hunger, not awe
- She should be funny about it: "So the inscrutable aliens want to talk to the teenager. Typical."
- She tells NO ONE — not because she agonizes about it, but because it doesn't occur to her. It's hers.

### Rewrite principles for C2:
- The revelation of the Whole should be terrifying AND fascinating
- The ultimatum should not be spelled out — it should arrive as understanding, the way you suddenly realize you've been standing in traffic
- Eszel's decision to leave should feel like NOT-deciding. She just... turns around. The way she leaned over the balcony — not a choice, an impulse
- "They would die in the middle of a committee meeting about evacuation procedures" — KEEP THIS LINE. It's perfect. It's the one moment of genuine Eszel voice in C2.
- Cut ALL italicized emphasis. Cut ALL triplet constructions.
- The committee meeting line aside, halve the length of Eszel's internal justification for leaving. Let the action speak.
